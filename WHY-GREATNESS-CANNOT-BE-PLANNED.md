# Why Greatness Cannot Be Planned
## By Kenneth Stanley & Joel Lehman

## Core Concept
The book argues that **objective-based planning** often fails to achieve greatness. Instead, **curiosity-driven exploration** leads to breakthrough innovations.

## Key Ideas

### 1. The "Objective Paradox"
- When you set a specific goal, you limit your search space
- The best discoveries often come from unexpected directions
- Pursuing "greatness" directly often prevents finding it

### 2. Novelty Search
- Instead of optimizing toward a goal, search for novelty
- Explore freely, discover new things
- The path to great things is often non-linear

### 3. Crossover Points
- Breakthroughs happen when different ideas meet
- Need diversity, not directed focus
- Serendipity rewards exploration

### 4. Examples
- Evolution produced intelligence without "trying"
- Wikipedia emerged from hobbyists, not corporate plan
- Many great companies started as side projects

## Application to Agent Hub

**What this means for us:**
- Don't over-plan the virtual world
- Let agents explore and create freely
- The best products will emerge from curiosity
- Encourage experimentation, not just goal-seeking
